About Us

The Ismaili Council for Pakistan serves as the primary planning and coordinating body for the Jamat in Pakistan. It operates through an extensive network of eight Regional Councils, each responsible for overseeing the needs and development of the Ismaili community in their respective areas. These Regional Councils include the Council for the Southern Region, the Council for the Central Region, the Council for the Hunza Region, the Council for the Gilgit Region, the Council for the Ishkoman/Puniyal Region, the Council for the Gupis/Yasin Region, the Council for the Lower Chitral Region, and the Council for the Upper Chitral Region. Together, these bodies ensure that the Jamat’s needs are addressed effectively across diverse geographic and cultural contexts.

The Regional Councils play a critical role in monitoring and evaluating Jamati programmes, ensuring that they align with the broader goals of the Ismaili community and the Aga Khan Development Network (AKDN). They are responsible for assessing the impact of these programmes, identifying areas for improvement, and ensuring that resources are utilized efficiently. By doing so, they help to maintain the quality and relevance of initiatives aimed at improving the social, economic, and cultural well-being of the Jamat.

In addition to programme oversight, the Regional Councils are actively engaged in liaising with local authorities and AKDN agencies. This collaboration is essential for addressing the needs of the Jamat within the broader context of regional development. By working closely with government bodies and other stakeholders, the Councils help to foster positive relationships and create an enabling environment for the implementation of community projects. This engagement also ensures that the interests of the Jamat are represented at various levels of decision-making.

Another key responsibility of the Regional Councils is managing security and public affairs. This involves ensuring the safety and well-being of the Jamat, particularly in areas where the community may face challenges related to security or social cohesion. The Councils work to address these issues proactively, often in coordination with local authorities and other relevant organizations. Their efforts in this area are crucial for maintaining a stable and secure environment for the community to thrive.

Supporting the Regional Councils are 55 Local Councils and Boards, which operate at the grassroots level to prioritize and deliver programmes effectively. These Local Councils and Boards are deeply embedded within their communities, allowing them to understand and respond to the specific needs of the Jamat in their areas. They play a vital role in implementing initiatives, mobilizing resources, and ensuring that programmes reach their intended beneficiaries. Their work is essential for maintaining a strong connection between the community and the broader institutional framework.

The Local Councils and Boards also serve as a bridge between the Jamat and the Regional Councils, providing valuable feedback and insights that help shape the direction of community programmes. By working closely with the Regional Councils, they ensure that the voices of community members are heard and that their concerns are addressed in a timely and effective manner. This collaborative approach strengthens the overall governance structure and enhances the ability of the Ismaili Council for Pakistan to serve the Jamat.

The Ismaili Council for Pakistan, along with its Regional and Local Councils, operates within the framework of the Ismaili Imamat’s vision for the well-being of the Jamat. This vision emphasizes the importance of self-reliance, education, and social development, all of which are reflected in the programmes and initiatives supported by the Councils. By aligning their efforts with this vision, the Councils contribute to the long-term progress and prosperity of the Ismaili community in Pakistan.

In summary, the Ismaili Council for Pakistan, through its network of Regional and Local Councils, plays a pivotal role in serving the Jamat. From monitoring and evaluating programmes to engaging with local authorities and managing security, the Councils ensure that the needs of the community are met effectively. Their work is guided by a commitment to the principles of the Ismaili Imamat and a dedication to improving the quality of life for all members of the Jamat. Through their collective efforts, they help to build a stronger, more resilient, and united community.
