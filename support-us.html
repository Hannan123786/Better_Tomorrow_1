Introduction to CFRC

Central Fund Raising Campaign (CFRC) is an initiative of the lsmaili Council for Pakistan that funds AKDN and Jamati programmes to fulfill Hazar Imam's vision of improving the quality of life of the Jamat and strengthening Imamat institutions. Started in 1999, CFRC supports programmes guided by Imamat's unique multi-input approach to development which not only covers issues related to health, education, habitat, natural disasters and economic development but also takes steps that focus on institutional development, youth empowerment, and artistic endeavours.

Hazar Imam explained this approach in a speech in 2002 in Amsterdam:
"Development is sustainable only if the beneficiaries become, in a gradual manner, the masters of the process. This means that initiatives cannot be contemplated exclusively in terms of economics, but rather as an integrated programme that encompasses social and cultural dimensions as well."

The Ismaili Imamat has endeavoured to realise the social conscience of Islam through institutional action.

"The ethics of Islam bridge the realms of faith on the one hand and practical life on the other-what we call Din and Dunya. Accordingly, my spiritual responsibilities for interpreting the faith are accompanied by a strong engagement in issues relating to the quality of life and wellbeing."
--- Mawlana Shah Karim, Paris, 15h June, 2007

CFRC Works on the core principle of contribution by all its participants based on their capacity to give on 'Hasbeh - Taufiq 'Hasbeh-Haysiyat'. In times of need, Imamat Institutional programmes are the pillar of strength. The generosity of the Jamat over the years has fueled many of these programmes to provide welfare for the less fortunate, increase access to quality education and healthcare, enhance professional skills. foster entrepreneurship, provide access to shelters, build infrastructure including irrigation and sanitation schemes, provide humanitarian assistance and prepare for disasters among other development activities. Every contribution counts to help the Jamat in need. The CFRC contributors, besides earning blessings and barakah for their generosity. are also blessed with an opportunity by Hazar Imam to participate towards the attainment of the Imamat's vision to improve quality of life of the Jamat. Contributions from CFRC helps to fund programmes in Education, Health, Habitat, Welfare, Economic Upliftment, Youth Empowerment and Institutional Strengthening. Ambitious goals have been set which the institutions aim to achieve with the generosity of the Jamat.

CFRC Plans

CFRC is an annual fundraising drive. Three types of plans have been designed to encourage maximum participation of the Jamat.

Conventional Plan:-
The conventional plan has a two-prong approach:-
  1. Pledge and contribute a fixed amount per month as a family.
  2. Pledge and contribute any amount as per your Niyat and make payments as per your convenience during the annual campaign period.
The popular wisdom, The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago; the second best time is now', recognises the need to nurture today for a Better Tomorrow; This approach is crucial to building a stronger, healthier, happier and safer Jamat. CFRC contributors are partners in the programmes of Imamat Institution and instrumental in their efforts to serve the Jamat. Their conviction and generousity provides encouragement and support to fulfill the vision of Hazar Imam for the Jamat.

Institutional Youth and Adult Plan:-
The Institutional Youth and Adult Plan has been designed to encourage those individuals 18 years of age and older giving khidmat at the institutions registered under the umbrella of the Council, such as Scouts, Guides, Rovers, Volunteers and other grassroot institutions, to participate in CFRC by pledging a unit of Rs. 500/- per month. The amount can be pooled with their fellow khidmatgars and donated as one unit.

Institutional Student Plan:-
Inculcating the notion of giving at an early age prepares the children of the Jamat to appreciate the act of giving and explore the thematic programmes being offered to the Jamat at large. Under the Institutional Student Plan, children up to 18 years of age giving khidmat at the institutions registered with the Council, like Shaheen Scouts, Girls Guides, Flute Band and other graceroot institutions, can contribute upto Rs. 100/- per month (maximum Rs. 1,200/- annually.) This tiny gesture today has huge implications for the future as it creates an act of giving and thoughtfuiness at an early age.
