Health

Aga khan Health Service, Pakistan {AKHS,P} aims to provide equitible, service oriented and innovative community based health services. It currently operates a network of 118 Basic Health Centres and eight Comprehensive Health Centres spread across Karachi, Sindh, Punjab,Gilgit-Baltistan and Chitral. ISO 9001:2008 certified, AKHS,P servers over 760,000 beneficiaries annually

Major initiatives include:
  • Accessibility and quality care services at health centres including improvment in facilities and equipment, training of staff and implementation of system
  • Outreach programme to support and strengthen hub and spokes model enhancing timely access to basic health services and specialised care
  • Social health protection through health insurance programmes to improve financial accessibility and affordability
  • implementation of health campaigns including screening of adults for early diagnasis and prevention of non-communicable diseases; palliative care staff training for improving end-of-life to patiens; and ECD activities assessing preatal & postnatal screening, growth monitoring, vaccination, nutrition counselling and development screening of children
  • Mobile Health (m-Health) and Electronic Health (e-Health) services to increase accessiblity to specialised care, improve care quality and reduce the cost of health care services
  • Development of Public Private Partnerships to provide services currently not avaliable in under-served areas

In 2015, Molana Shah Karim graciously approved the formation of the Aga Khan Health Board for Pakistan(AKHB,P), with the mandate to improve the health of the Jamat. One of its key roles is the promotion of healthy lifestyles through advocacy and by using a model of behavior change communication

Major initiatives include:

  • Comprehensive sessions and screening programmes for the bJamat with refrral services convernig non-communicable and communicable diseases, adolescence health, mental health and ECD health
  • Capacity building of volunteer health workers and implementation of systems for effective referrals.
  • Family health insurance scheme to help provide affordable coverage to the Jamat

    • Education

    Since the first Aga Khan School was established in 1905 in Gwadar, Aga Khan Education Service, Pakistan (AKES,P) has worked to ensure that students of all ages have access to quality learning opportunities. It operates programmes and institutions which span the educational ladder, from early childhood programmes to primary and secondary schools. Over 40,000 students a year benefit from 163 facilities including schools and hostels with a strength of more than 2,700 employees most of whom are teachers.

    Major initiatives include:
    • Expansion of Early Childhood Development (ECD) programmes.
    • Upgradation and extension of schools to increase access to higher level education.
    • Continuous enhancement in quality of teaching through teacher training and implementation of information systems.
    • Building access through development and rollout of innovative curriculum customised to local environments.
    • Affiliating more schools with Aga Khan University Examination Board (AKU-EB) to enhance learning and performance of students.
    • Rollout of construction and improvement of school and hostel physical infrastructure for conducive learning and safety.

    In 2015, Mawlana Shah Karim graciously approved the formation of a separate education board, Aga Khan Education Board for Pakistan (AKEB,P) to promote equitable access to education for the Jamat. It aims to foster excellence through implementation of strategies to monitor and improve standards of education and academic achievement of children and adults.

    Major initiatives include:

  • Supporting expansion of ECD including awareness activities, scale-up of Parwaaz (parental education programme for children six months to three years) and mobilisation to enrol in AKES,P and other quality ECD programmes.
  • Supporting access to community-based schools in Jamati areas and quality residential facilities for secondary and post-secondary education.
  • Offering bursaries, scholarships and grants and promoting access to other financial aid services for education to deserving students.
  • Guidance and counselling to students for improving access to good quality post-secondary and tertiary education.
  • Career advisory services to help students make informed career decisions.
  • Continuing education programmes with focus on improving English language proficiency in the Jamat across all age groups.

    • Habitat

    The Aga Khan Agency for Habitat (AKAH) was formed by bringing together five initiatives of the Aga Khan Development Network to synergistically approach the issues of disaster risk and climate change. As a result, the Aga Khan Planning and Building Service and Focus Humanitarian Assistance have been integrated into a single entity: AKAH. The mandate of AKAH includes improving the quality of life of vulnerable communities in Pakistan through disaster preparedness, response and mitigation activities, planning and implementation of built environment improvement related development initiatives. AKAH strives to create habitats that are safer, economically viable, face minimum level of natural disaster and are environmentally, economically and socially appropriate.

    Major initiatives include:
    • Emergency preparedness and response to growing natural disasters including comprehensive Hazard, Vuinerability and Risk Assessments, installation of early warning systems, maintenance of emergency stockpiles and equipment, search and rescue, and ongoing training of volunteers and institutions.
    • Safer habitat including safe and energy efficient buildings, strengthening of community physical infrastructure and protection against natural disasters using modern engineering and architectural solutions.
    • Rehabilitation and settlement planning including construction of temporary shelters for populations displaced as a result of disasters.
    • Water and sanitation programmes to provide safe drinking water supply and household sanitation infrastructure.
    • Climate change mitigation and adaptation activities such as exposure to new agriculture techniques, creating alternative livelihood opportunities, improving governance of natural resources and reducing risk to natural hazards.
